Introduction

Agriculture refers to the cultivation of crops and the raising of animals to provide essential products such as food, fiber, and fuel. It supports the global population and plays a critical role in ensuring food security and sustainability. However, the sector faces numerous challenges including climate change, pest infestations, and inefficient farming practices that hinder productivity and sustainability.

Current agricultural challenges include the under-utilization of natural resources, crop mismanagement, and wasteful practices. For instance, biomass from timber harvesting could be repurposed for bio-energy, composting, or construction, promoting sustainability and reducing waste. Similarly, innovations like improved seedling assessment methods can save farmers time and labor, ultimately enhancing yields.

Initiatives

Addressing these challenges requires innovative solutions and research. For example, studies at the university include utilizing bamboo leaf briquettes and exploring bamboo shoots for various applications. Other projects involve employing image processing systems to evaluate seed quality and analyzing mushrooms for their nutraceutical potential.

Research has also focused on alternative uses for local resources like banana production and water lilies, assessing their potential as livestock feed extenders or raw materials for handicrafts. Further studies have explored indigenous plants for pest control and the use of plant extracts as eco-friendly agricultural solutions. Additionally, experiments on tamarind orchards examined nutrient correlations and tree development to optimize fruit production.

R4D Thrusts and Priorities

  • Develop innovative pest management strategies, particularly for the Golden Apple Snail.
  • Enhance science-based production management strategies for agriculture.
  • Explore bamboo's full potential for sustainable technologies and waste minimization.
  • Maximize banana use as feed extenders and materials for handicrafts.
  • Promote food security and sustainability through diverse plant and tree-based food products.

Recommendations

  • Adopt modern agricultural technologies such as water management systems and plant-monitoring tools to increase efficiency and reduce labor costs.
  • Focus on innovative uses of agricultural waste, including natural pest controllers and eco-friendly products, to support sustainable farming practices.
